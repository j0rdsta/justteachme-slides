Just Teach Me

Helping the technologically-challenged with smart phones

Created by Tommi Sullivan, Joshua Holmes, David Farrow and Jordan Gardiner

A growing number of people struggle with the basics of how to use their smart phone.

Landline telephones are on-the-decline, and everyone's being forced onto smart phones

One-third of Australians don't have a landline phone at home (Haptic Generation, 2015)

People look for help through:

  • The telco they are with
  • Family - especially the youth
  • Online - but most resources are overly technical

This is JustTeachMe

A learning platform to teach people how to use their smartphones

Suitable for all kinds of cognitive learning styles:

  • Visual & Kinesthetic learners - interactive screenshots on every step: 'click the Call button to proceed'
  • Auditory learners - voice-overs

Available now on Google Play
iOS version coming soon

Our target market says:
YES PLEASE!

Youth won't have to be bugged by their parents/grandparents ever again for technical issues

We surveyed young people at Robina Town Centre yesterday - over 60% said they would like to teach their parents and grandparents how to use their phone

Telcos can resolve customer problems easier

We also surveyed staff members and managers from Optus, Telstra, Virgin and Vodafone - all of them would like to install this on their customers' phones!

Monetization

In-app purchases: Phone functionality lessons are free, app specific lessons cost

Alternatively thinking about ad-supported model: most people at Robina Town Centre and through Google Consumer Surveys wanted this

Opportunity for companies to sponsor lessons: Facebook wants the population of the Earth on their social network, and the elderly are the least likely to use Facebook - fix that with lessons

Potential to sell to Telcos: co-branding, pre-install it on Android phones

We can save Australian telcos approx. $16,224,250 per year in lost time for these issues
